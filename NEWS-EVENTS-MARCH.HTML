FEBRUARY 2023 NEWS AND EVENTS UPDATES

MarkJunior School Swimmers Make a Splash at Regional Competition On Saturday, March 12th, MarkJunior School's swim team competed in the regional swimming competition at the local pool. The event featured over 20 schools from the region, and MarkJunior School's swimmers made an impressive showing, with several students winning medals. In the girls' category, Emma Smith, a 9th-grade student, won first place in the 50-meter freestyle event, and second place in the 100-meter butterfly event. Meanwhile, in the boys' category, John Lee, an 8th-grade student, won first place in the 100-meter backstroke event, and second place in the 50-meter freestyle event. Overall, MarkJunior School's swim team performed well in the competition, with many students achieving personal bests and demonstrating excellent sportsmanship. The school's swimming coach, Coach Johnson, expressed pride in the team's performance, saying, "I am incredibly proud of the effort and dedication that our swimmers showed in this competition. They trained hard and gave it their all, and their results speak for themselves." The competition was attended by many parents and supporters, who cheered on the swimmers throughout the event. According to one parent, "It was so exciting to see our school's swimmers competing with such enthusiasm and determination. They represented our school so well, and it was great to see them achieving such great results."
