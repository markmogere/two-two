APRIL 2023 NEWS AND EVENTS UPDATES

On Tuesday, April 5th, a group of students from MarkJunior School embarked on an exciting field trip to Nairobi National Park. The trip was organized by the school's biology department as part of the school's commitment to providing students with hands-on learning experiences that enhance their understanding of the natural world. The students, accompanied by their biology teacher, Ms. Kimani, arrived at the park early in the morning, where they were greeted by a knowledgeable guide who led them on a safari through the park. The students were able to observe a wide range of wildlife, including lions, zebras, giraffes, and elephants, in their natural habitats. During the safari, the guide provided the students with interesting information about the park's ecosystem, the behavior of the animals, and the importance of conservation efforts. The students were fascinated by the diversity and complexity of the natural world, and were eager to learn more about the park's flora and fauna. After the safari, the group had the opportunity to visit the park's animal orphanage, where they were able to interact with young animals that had been rescued from various parts of the country. The students were delighted to feed and play with the animals, and were impressed by the dedication and hard work of the park's staff in caring for them. According to Ms. Kimani, "The trip was a fantastic learning experience for our students. They were able to see the importance of conservation efforts firsthand and learn about the intricate web of relationships that exists in the natural world. We are grateful to Nairobi National Park for giving our students this opportunity." The trip was a great success, and the students returned to school with a newfound appreciation for the natural world and a commitment to protecting it. The school community looks forward to future field trips that will enable students to further explore and understand the world around them.
